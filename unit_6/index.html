Aroids on Steroids

AR·OID (/ˈeroid/) :

The Araceae are a family of monocotyledonous flowering plants in which flowers are borne on a type of inflorescence called a spadix. The spadix is usually accompanied by, and sometimes partially enclosed in, a spathe or leaf-like bract. Also known as the arum family, members are often colloquially known as aroids.

Fact of the Day:

Aroids have different types of roots adapted to their purpose. They have different adventitious roots for climbing, attaching to rocks or taking in water. Many tropical species have shiny leaves to deter the mosses and lichens that grow in abundance in the rainforest
